5 Money Managing Tips

By: Jainam Patel

Published on 9/15/24

Managing your money wisely is a skill that will serve you for life. If you want to take control of your finances, here are five money management tips for teens to get started:

1: Set Financial Goals

Whether you're saving for a new Iphone, car, or even college, having a goal in mind will keep you motivated. Break down your goals into short-term and long-term, and track your progress to stay on course.

2: Use a Budgeting App

There are plenty of apps designed to help teens track spending and savings. Apps like Mint or YNAB (You Need A Budget) allow you to create categories for your expenses and ensure you're not overspending in one area. You can also go old school and write everything in a journal or even a Google Doc.

3: Save a Portion of All Income

Get into the habit of saving a percentage of all the money you earn or receive as gifts. A good rule is to save at least 20% of your income. You’ll build a cushion for future expenses or emergencies.

4: Open a Bank Account

If you don’t have one yet, opening a savings and/or checking account is a great way to manage your money. Many banks offer special teen accounts with no fees, and it’s a great introduction to using digital banking tools like mobile deposits and tracking transactions.

5: Learn About Investing Early

It’s never too early to start learning about investing. While saving is crucial, investing can help grow your money over time. Explore beginner investment platforms like Fidelity Youth Account or Acorns to start with small amounts.
